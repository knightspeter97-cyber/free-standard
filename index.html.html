The Free Standard Exists Because the Truth Needs a Home

For decades, the American conservative has been handed a script. Be angry at the right people. Fear the right enemies. Vote for the right candidates. And in exchange, the machine will protect your values.

It hasn't. Not because conservatism failed — but because the institutions that claimed to represent it were captured by the same donor class, the same defense contractors, and the same globalist consensus they claimed to oppose.

The Free Standard was built for the American who is done being managed. For the father who wants to raise his children with Christian values and not apologize for it. For the veteran who is tired of watching the country he fought for be dismantled by people who never served. For the citizen who has read enough history to know that what we are being told does not match what has actually happened.

"It is the first responsibility of every citizen to question authority."
— Benjamin Franklin

We are not here to tell you what to think. We are here to give you the information, the questions, and the historical context to think for yourself — anchored in Christian faith, American founding principles, and the kind of honest inquiry that the bought press has abandoned.

We believe in free thought. We believe in faith. We believe in freedom. And we believe those three things are inseparable.

Who We Are

About The Free Standard

Built by Americans who got tired of waiting for someone else to say what needed to be said.

This Started With a Simple Question: Why Aren't They Asking This?

The Free Standard began when a group of ordinary Americans — veterans, fathers, business owners, pastors, and citizens — kept noticing the same thing: the questions that mattered most were never getting asked by the people who were supposed to be asking them.

Not by the mainstream press. Not by the establishment conservative media. Not by the politicians who claimed to represent them. Every channel was pointed in the same direction, funded by the same interests, reading from the same script.

So we built something different.

The Free Standard is an independent media platform — no corporate ownership, no advertiser conflicts of interest, no donor class to answer to. We produce a weekly podcast, original reporting, and guest commentary, all anchored in the conviction that the American people deserve honest journalism and are capable of handling the truth.

We are not a political party. We are not a PAC. We are not aligned with any political figure or faction. We are a publication — in the oldest and most American sense of that word. We print what we believe. We invite you to read, listen, question, and decide for yourself.

Editorial Independence

The Free Standard accepts no advertising from defense contractors, pharmaceutical companies, financial institutions, or political organizations. Our funding comes entirely from readers and listeners who support independent journalism. That's it.

Our Standard for Truth

Every claim we publish is sourced. We cite primary documents when they exist. We distinguish between opinion and reporting. We correct errors publicly and promptly. We ask you to hold us to this standard — and to hold everyone else to it too.

Our Faith

We are a publication with a Christian worldview. This does not mean we are a church bulletin or a denominational organ. It means we believe truth is not relative, that moral clarity is possible, and that free thought anchored in faith produces better citizens than ideology divorced from it.

What We Are Not

We are not spokesmen for the Republican Party. We are not aligned with any political figure. We will criticize any politician, institution, or media outlet — regardless of party — when they fail the American people. That includes the neoconservative establishment that has hijacked the conservative movement.
